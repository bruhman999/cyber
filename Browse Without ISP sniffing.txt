OPTION 2 — TOR BROWSER (Strong Anonymity, Slow)
----------------------------------------------------------------
How it works:
  Your Device → Node 1 → Node 2 → Node 3 → Website
  Each node only knows the previous and next hop
  Nobody knows both who you are AND what you visit

  ISP sees: "User connected to Tor network"
  ISP cannot see: What sites you visit

How to use:
  1. Download Tor Browser from torproject.org
  2. Install and open it
  3. Browse normally — no configuration needed

  ✅ Free
  ✅ Very strong anonymity
  ✅ Built in ECH and DNS over Tor
  ❌ Very slow (traffic bounces through 3 nodes)
  ❌ Some sites block Tor exit nodes
  ❌ ISP can see you USE Tor (not what you visit)
  ❌ Not good for streaming or large downloads

To hide that you use Tor from ISP:
  Use Tor Bridges (obfs4):
  1. Open Tor Browser
  2. Connection settings → Use a bridge
  3. Select obfs4
  4. ISP just sees random encrypted traffic

----------------------------------------------------------------
OPTION 4 — I2P (Invisible Internet Project)
----------------------------------------------------------------
Alternative to Tor, designed for internal anonymous network

  ✅ Highly anonymous
  ✅ Decentralized
  ❌ Harder to set up
  ❌ Mostly for accessing I2P sites (.i2p domains)
  ❌ Not great for regular web browsing
  ❌ Small user base

----------------------------------------------------------------
OPTION 5 — RESIDENTIAL PROXY
----------------------------------------------------------------
Routes your traffic through another person's real home IP

  ✅ Looks like normal home traffic to ISP
  ✅ Very hard to detect
  ❌ Costs money
  ❌ Slower than direct connection
  ❌ Trust issues with proxy provider

IMPORTANT THINGS TO UNDERSTAND
----------------------------------------------------------------
1. NO method is 100% anonymous
   - Your device, browser, and behavior can still
     identify you (browser fingerprinting)
   - Logging into Google/Facebook etc immediately
     identifies you regardless of VPN or Tor

2. VPNs shift trust dont eliminate it
   - Instead of ISP seeing your traffic
     the VPN provider sees it
   - Choose a VPN with a no-logs policy
   - Windscribe has a no-logs policy ✅

3. Browser fingerprinting can still track you
   - Your browser leaks: screen size, fonts,
     plugins, timezone, language etc
   - Fix: Use Firefox with arkenfox user.js
     or use Tor Browser

4. Metadata is still visible
   - Even with VPN+Tor ISP sees:
     How much data you transfer
     When you are online
     That you use a VPN or Tor
